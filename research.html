Clinical Research & Evidence

Scientific validation of PDT effectiveness

Groundbreaking India Clinical Trial

Low-Cost PDT System for Early Oral Cancer (2022)

Published: Photodiagnosis and Photodynamic Therapy, JN Medical College, Aligarh, India

Study Highlights:

  • 30 patients with T1N0M0 buccal mucosal cancer treated successfully
  • Portable, battery-powered LED system at 635nm wavelength
  • Smartphone-based fluorescence imaging for treatment guidance
  • Reached rural patients up to 200 km from medical center
  • Complete tumor response with photobleaching correlation

This study demonstrated that low-cost, portable PDT can effectively treat early oral cancer in resource-limited settings, making treatment accessible to underserved populations.

Meta-Analysis of PDT for Oral Precancerous Lesions

Multiple studies analyzed through 2021

Key Findings:

  • Overall complete response rate: 52% (CI: 0.36-0.68)
  • Partial response rate: 82% (CI: 0.74-0.88)
  • 20% ALA showed 68% complete response - most effective
  • Lesion size decreased by average 1.38 cm² after PDT

Advanced Oral Cancer Treatment Success

Lambert et al., Clinical studies

In patients with advanced oral cancer who refused or were unsuitable for conventional treatments:

  • 76.9% achieved complete response
  • 42.3% achieved local tumor control
  • PDT used as palliative treatment with excellent outcomes
