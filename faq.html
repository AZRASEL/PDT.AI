Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers about PDT

PDT is generally well-tolerated with minimal discomfort. Most patients experience only mild sensation during treatment.

Complete PDT takes 4-6 hours including drug accumulation time. Light delivery is 15-30 minutes. It's an outpatient procedure.

Mild temporary effects: light sensitivity (1-2 days), mild swelling, temporary eating discomfort. Much fewer effects than surgery or chemotherapy.

Very effective for early-stage cancers: 52-76% complete response. For precancerous lesions, 82% show improvement. Best when detected early.

Yes! PDT can be safely repeated without cumulative toxic effects. Unlike radiation, there's no lifetime dose limit. Ideal for recurrent lesions.

PDT preserves speech, swallowing, and taste! Only temporary mild eating discomfort during healing (7-17 days). Normal functions are maintained.

Costs vary by treatment size. Generally more cost-effective than surgery or extended chemo/radiation. Contact us for consultation and pricing.
